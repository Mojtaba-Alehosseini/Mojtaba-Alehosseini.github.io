About

M.Sc. student in Computer Science (AI) at the University of Genova. Before Italy I spent two years as a Business Technology Analyst in Iran, shipping Power BI dashboards across sales, finance, and supply chain — the kind of work where a 25% cut in reporting time is the difference between a meeting that runs over and one that ends on time.

The longer story is hardware. Between 2020 and 2024 I co-built and operated Riggosaurus, a small distributed GPU compute operation: 20+ rigs, 100+ cards, six operating systems, six wallets. We did everything ourselves — PSU sizing, PCIe-riser topology, silicon-level overclocking, remote monitoring, multi-wallet accounting. No templates, no outsourcing. The operation ran for 28 months and wound down after Ethereum's Proof-of-Stake transition.

I'm now using the Master's at Genova to move into AI/ML — currently working through LLMs and retrieval-augmented systems, classical ML, and computer vision. Coming from BI, I think about models in terms of the dashboard they have to show up on, not just the validation loss curve.

I learned to measure whether something is actually working in dollars-per-watt, not in vibes.

Based in Genoa, Italy. Open to AI/ML internships and graduate roles across the EU starting in 2026/2027. Visa sponsorship welcome.
